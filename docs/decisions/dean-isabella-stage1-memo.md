To: CFO

From: Financial Analyst

Subject: Value of Accounting Ratio Analysis


Company Overview:

This analysis will focus on Nike, Inc., a leading company in the athletic apparel and footwear industry. Nike designs, markets, and sells sportswear products globally, with a strong emphasis on brand innovation and direct-to-consumer sales.

Why Ratio Analysis Matters:

Financial statements provide important data, but they can be difficult to interpret on their own. Ratio analysis helps translate that data into clear insights by showing relationships between key financial elements. It allows us to evaluate how well the company is performing, how efficiently it operates, how much financial risk it carries, and whether it can meet short-term obligations. This makes it easier to identify trends over time and compare Nike’s performance to competitors, ultimately supporting better strategic and financial decisions.

Six Ratio Categories:

This analysis will include six main categories to give a well-rounded view of the company:

-Performance Ratios: Measure overall returns generated for shareholders.

-Profitability Ratios: Show how effectively the company turns revenue into profit.

-Efficiency Ratios: Evaluate how well assets are used to generate sales.

-Leverage Ratios: Assess the company’s use of debt and financial risk.

-Liquidity Ratios: Indicate the company’s ability to meet short-term obligations.

-DuPont Analysis: Breaks down return on equity into key drivers for deeper understanding.

Data Sources:

Financial data will be collected from reliable public sources, including Nike’s SEC 10-K filings (EDGAR database), the company’s Investor Relations website, and platforms such as Yahoo Finance for additional reference.
